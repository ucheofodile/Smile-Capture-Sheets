Core Message
Spine
We all carry assumptions about what we want that we don't discover until reality tests them. Three stories — Uche's own hesitation over a CEO role offered in the "wrong" country, a colleague who insisted for years he had no interest in international experience or a senior promotion and then got both within a year, and a third leader who was completely deliberate and transparent about his career plan and still didn't get the role it was all built toward — are connected not by sharing an ending or a lesson, but by the fact that in each case, reality tested a theory the person didn't know they were holding.
The Distinction
This deliberately refuses to resolve into a leadership lesson. The episode names this directly mid-recording: the third story exists specifically to stop the episode from collapsing into "be flexible and say yes more often." Ends on discovery, not advice.
Operating Principle
There isn't one, by design. Certainty about what matters, what we want, or what will make a difference is often untested — and reality periodically exposes the gap, with no guarantee of resolution either way.
The Cost
A leader who hasn't examined their own terms and conditions risks two failure modes: rejecting a real opportunity because it doesn't arrive in the expected package, or staying certain about a refusal for years without noticing it was never load-bearing. The third story shows the cost cuts the other way too — even transparent, well-reasoned planning carries no guarantee.

Edit Instructions
Overall Instruction
The single highest-stakes moment in this edit is the transition out of Story 1. The thumbnail hooks people on the DRC story, and the only thing that stops them leaving once that story resolves is whether this transition still lands: "Apparently, I had terms and conditions. Who knew?" into "I'd never asked myself, like, but where?" That is the line where the listener stops listening for DRC and starts listening for themselves. Do not tighten or rush this for pacing — if it gets cut close, the episode plays as a self-contained anecdote and people log off before Story 2 starts.

Wondering, not explaining. Take 2 has this; Take 1 doesn't. Don't tighten pacing in a way that makes it sound more like a resolved argument.

Episode Description
Uche tells three stories about discovering hidden assumptions the hard way: her own disappointment when offered the CEO role she'd always wanted — just not in the country she'd pictured; a colleague who spent years certain he had no interest in international work or a senior promotion, then got both within a year of saying yes once; and a third leader whose career plan was completely deliberate and transparent, who still didn't get the role the whole plan was built toward. The episode resists turning into a tidy leadership lesson on purpose — it ends on discovery, not advice.
Key Themes
